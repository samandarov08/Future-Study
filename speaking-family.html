👨‍👩‍👧 Oila

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
Can you describe your family?
👁 Ko'rish
I come from a close-knit extended family. We live with my parents and grandparents, which means there are always people around to support each other. My family has very strong values centred around respect, education and togetherness. Each member plays an important role in our household.
💡 Maslahat: Use descriptive adjectives: close-knit, supportive, tight-knit
2
Who in your family has influenced you the most?
👁 Ko'rish
Without doubt, my mother has been my greatest role model. Her dedication to our upbringing, her sacrifices for the family, and her unwavering positivity have shaped who I am today. She taught me the importance of responsibility and the value of hard work through her own example.
💡 Maslahat: Be specific and emotional — this makes answers memorable
3
How often do you spend time with your family?
👁 Ko'rish
We have dinner together every evening, which I consider very important for maintaining our family bond. On weekends, we often visit relatives or have larger reunions with our extended family. These gatherings are something I genuinely cherish — they connect different generations and keep our traditions alive.
💡 Maslahat: Use time expressions: daily, regularly, on weekends, occasionally
4
Do you think family is more important than friends?
👁 Ko'rish
I think it's difficult to compare the two — they fulfil different needs. Family provides unconditional support and a sense of belonging, while friends offer companionship and shared experiences. Both are essential. However, family relationships often have a deeper, more lasting impact on our character and values.
💡 Maslahat: Avoid being too absolute — 'it depends' shows maturity
5
Are family structures in your country changing?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, significantly. Traditionally, extended families living together were common in Uzbekistan. Today, particularly in cities, nuclear families are becoming more typical as young people value independence. However, strong family bonds and respect for elders remain deeply rooted in our culture.
💡 Maslahat: Describe change: 'traditionally... today, however'
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a family member you admire

  • Who this person is
  • What they do or have done
  • What qualities they have
  • Why you admire them
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I would like to talk about my grandfather, who is perhaps the most admirable person I have ever known. He grew up in very difficult circumstances but managed to build a successful life through sheer determination and hard work. What I admire most about him is his wisdom — he has an extraordinary ability to see the bigger picture in any situation and offer calm, thoughtful advice. He has always been a tremendous role model for our entire family, embodying values of honesty, responsibility and compassion. Despite the sacrifices he made for his family over the decades, he never complains and always maintains a positive outlook on life. Spending time with him is a privilege — his stories connect me to our history and remind me of what truly matters. I hope to carry the values and wisdom he has passed down through generations forward in my own life.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How have family roles changed in modern society?
👁 Ko'rish
Family roles have shifted considerably. In the past, there was a clear division — men worked and women managed the home. Today, both parents typically work and share domestic responsibilities. Children are encouraged to develop independence earlier, and grandparents are less likely to live in the same household. These changes reflect broader shifts in social and economic life.
💡 Maslahat: Use past vs present structure clearly
2
Do you think it is better for children to grow up in large or small families?
👁 Ko'rish
Both have their advantages. Children in large families learn important social skills — sharing, compromise, and building strong bonds. However, smaller families can provide more individual attention and resources for each child. The quality of parenting and the values instilled are arguably more important than family size.
💡 Maslahat: Compare both + give your conclusion
3
Should elderly parents live with their adult children or independently?
👁 Ko'rish
This is a very personal and cultural question. In Uzbekistan, living with elderly parents is both a tradition and a deeply valued responsibility. It provides emotional support and strengthens generational bonds. However, some elderly people prefer their independence. The ideal solution depends on the family's circumstances and the wishes of the elderly person themselves.
💡 Maslahat: Respect cultural context + present multiple views
