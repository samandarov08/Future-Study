💼 Ish va Kelajak

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
What kind of job would you like to have in the future?
👁 Ko'rish
I aspire to work in the technology sector, ideally as a software developer or in a role that combines creativity with technical skills. What matters most to me is job satisfaction — I want a career where I can keep learning, solve real problems, and see the impact of my work. A good work-life balance is also very important to me.
💡 Maslahat: Use aspirational language: 'I aspire to', 'my goal is', 'ideally'
2
What job did you want to do when you were a child?
👁 Ko'rish
Like many children, I went through several phases. I wanted to be a doctor for a while because I admired the idea of helping people. Later I became fascinated by technology and computers. Looking back, even though my ambitions changed, they always reflected a desire to do something meaningful and make a positive contribution.
💡 Maslahat: Use past tense + reflection: 'looking back'
3
Is it important to enjoy your work?
👁 Ko'rish
Absolutely. Research consistently shows that people who find meaning in their work are significantly more productive and creative. Job satisfaction also reduces stress and burnout. While financial stability is important, spending 8 hours a day doing something you dislike is ultimately unsustainable for both mental health and career growth.
💡 Maslahat: Make a strong argument with evidence
4
Would you prefer to work for a company or be self-employed?
👁 Ko'rish
I think I would ultimately prefer freelancing or running my own small business. Being an entrepreneur offers creative freedom, flexibility, and the satisfaction of building something of your own. However, I recognise that starting out in a company is valuable for gaining experience, mentorship, and professional networking.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge both options before giving preference
5
How do you feel about doing an internship before working full-time?
👁 Ko'rish
Internships are invaluable. They bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and real-world experience. Even unpaid or short-term internships teach workplace etiquette, how to meet deadlines, and how to collaborate professionally. I firmly believe every student should gain some work experience before graduating.
💡 Maslahat: Use strong positive language: 'invaluable', 'essential', 'bridge the gap'
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe your ideal job or career

  • What the job is
  • What skills it requires
  • What you would do in this job
  • Why it appeals to you
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
My ideal job would be working as a software developer or digital product designer for a forward-thinking technology company. This role requires a combination of technical skills — programming, problem-solving, and data analysis — as well as creativity and strong communication abilities. In this position, I would spend my days designing and building digital products that genuinely improve people's lives, collaborating with a diverse team and continuously learning new technologies. What particularly appeals to me about this career is the perfect balance it offers between creativity and logic. No two projects are the same, which means the work remains stimulating and challenging. Additionally, the technology sector offers excellent opportunities for promotion and professional growth. I am also drawn to the possibility of eventually working remotely or even starting my own venture as an entrepreneur, which would give me the ultimate freedom to pursue projects I am truly passionate about.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How has the nature of work changed in the modern world?
👁 Ko'rish
Work has been transformed by technology. Remote work has become standard for many industries, blurring the boundaries between professional and personal life. Automation has replaced routine jobs while creating new roles requiring advanced skills. The traditional concept of a stable, lifelong job with one employer has largely disappeared, replaced by more flexible, project-based freelance arrangements.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'has been transformed', 'has replaced', 'has largely disappeared'
2
What are the biggest challenges young people face when entering the job market?
👁 Ko'rish
The most significant challenge is the skills gap — many graduates find their academic qualifications don't fully match what employers need. Unemployment among young people is persistently high in many countries. Additionally, the expectation of immediate experience creates a paradox — you need experience to get a job, but you need a job to gain experience. Networking and internships help bridge this gap.
💡 Maslahat: Identify specific challenges with clear explanations
3
Do you think work-life balance is achievable in today's world?
👁 Ko'rish
Work-life balance is increasingly difficult to achieve in an always-connected digital world. The expectation to respond to messages outside working hours has intensified. However, I believe it is achievable with clear boundaries and the right workplace culture. Companies that prioritise employee wellbeing tend to have higher productivity and lower turnover — so good balance actually benefits employers too.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge the difficulty + offer a solution
