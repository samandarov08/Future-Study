📚 Kitob va Kino

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
Do you prefer reading books or watching films?
👁 Ko'rish
I enjoy both for different reasons. Books allow deeper immersion — you can explore a character's inner thoughts in ways that films rarely capture. However, a well-crafted film with a powerful soundtrack can create an emotional experience that is uniquely cinematic. If I had to choose, I'd say books, because they develop imagination and vocabulary more effectively.
💡 Maslahat: Compare clearly + give a preference with reasoning
2
What kind of books do you usually read?
👁 Ko'rish
I mainly read non-fiction — books about technology, psychology and history. I find them more immediately applicable to real life. However, I do enjoy fiction occasionally, particularly historical novels where an engaging narrative brings real events to life. I've recently switched to e-books for convenience.
💡 Maslahat: Be specific about genres and give a reason
3
What was the last film you watched? Did you enjoy it?
👁 Ko'rish
The last film I watched was a critically acclaimed documentary about artificial intelligence. I found it absolutely fascinating — it explored complex ethical questions in a very accessible way. The narrative structure was compelling, moving between expert interviews and vivid visual storytelling. I would recommend it to anyone interested in technology and its social impact.
💡 Maslahat: Describe the film + say WHY you liked/disliked it
4
Do you think reading is becoming less popular?
👁 Ko'rish
Unfortunately, yes. With the rise of streaming platforms and social media, many people — especially younger generations — read far less than previous ones. However, e-books and audiobooks have attracted new readers who prefer digital formats. I remain optimistic that reading will survive, though its form continues to evolve.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge the trend + offer a counterpoint
5
Do you prefer to watch films at the cinema or at home?
👁 Ko'rish
For major releases — big blockbusters or films with spectacular visuals and soundtracks — the cinema is incomparable. The shared experience of watching with an audience adds something special. However, for documentaries or smaller, more intimate films, watching at home via streaming is perfectly comfortable and far more convenient.
💡 Maslahat: Distinguish between types of films + justify
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a book or film that had a strong effect on you

  • What the book/film is
  • What it is about
  • When you read/watched it
  • Why it affected you strongly
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I would like to talk about a book called 'The Alchemist' by Paulo Coelho, which had a profound effect on me when I read it a couple of years ago. The book follows the journey of a young shepherd named Santiago who travels across the world in pursuit of his dream. The central theme is the importance of following one's true purpose in life and trusting the journey, even when it is difficult or uncertain. What struck me most was how the narrative managed to feel both universal and deeply personal at the same time. Several passages resonated with me so strongly that I found myself rereading them multiple times. The book arrived at a moment in my life when I was feeling uncertain about my future direction, and it genuinely helped me refocus. It reminded me that the path to any meaningful goal requires courage, persistence, and an openness to unexpected experiences. I've since recommended it to several friends, and it continues to be a book I return to when I need perspective and motivation.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
Do you think books or films are more influential in shaping society?
👁 Ko'rish
Both have significant cultural influence, but I believe books have historically been more transformative — major philosophical and political movements have been driven by written works. However, in contemporary society, films and streaming platforms arguably reach larger audiences more quickly. A powerful documentary can shift public opinion on major issues almost overnight.
💡 Maslahat: Historical vs contemporary — use this contrast
2
Should all books be available digitally and for free?
👁 Ko'rish
While universal access to knowledge is a noble goal, making all books freely available would undermine authors' livelihoods. A middle ground exists — libraries, both physical and digital, provide free access while still compensating creators. E-books have already made reading far more affordable; the challenge is ensuring that digital access doesn't replace physical book culture entirely.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge the ideal + identify practical challenges
3
How do films and books reflect changes in society?
👁 Ko'rish
Literature and cinema are mirrors of their time. The themes, characters and genres that dominate popular culture reveal society's preoccupations, anxieties and values. For example, the surge in dystopian fiction reflects contemporary concerns about technology and democracy. Historical periods of social upheaval consistently produce some of the most powerful and enduring artistic works.
💡 Maslahat: Use specific examples to illustrate your point
