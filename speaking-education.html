🎓 Ta'lim

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
What do you enjoy most about studying?
👁 Ko'rish
What I enjoy most is the feeling of understanding something new. When a difficult concept finally clicks, it's genuinely satisfying. I also appreciate the opportunity to develop critical thinking skills through challenging assignments. Peer learning — discussing ideas with classmates — is something I find particularly valuable.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'what I enjoy most is', 'I particularly appreciate'
2
Do you prefer studying alone or with others?
👁 Ko'rish
It depends on the task. For memorisation and focused reading, I prefer studying alone in a quiet environment. However, for complex topics that require discussion and debate, studying with others is far more effective. Peer learning often helps me see different perspectives I wouldn't have considered on my own.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'it depends' + explain both sides
3
Is education in your country mostly free or do students pay?
👁 Ko'rish
In Uzbekistan, there is a mix of state-funded and fee-paying places at universities. Students with high academic results can win scholarship places and study for free, while others pay tuition fees. Compulsory education at school level is free for everyone, which I think is very important for equal opportunity.
💡 Maslahat: Be specific about your country's system
4
What subject did you enjoy most at school and why?
👁 Ko'rish
I particularly enjoyed mathematics because it requires logical thinking and gives clear, definitive answers. I also appreciated how it was connected to real life — from calculating distances to understanding data. It's a discipline that trains the mind to think systematically and solve problems efficiently.
💡 Maslahat: Give reason + real-world connection
5
How important is it to continue learning after school?
👁 Ko'rish
Extremely important. The world changes so rapidly that formal education alone is no longer sufficient. Distance learning platforms and online courses have made lifelong learning accessible to everyone. I regularly use online resources to expand my knowledge beyond the curriculum.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'lifelong learning', 'continuous development'
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a teacher who has influenced you greatly

  • Who this teacher is
  • What subject they taught
  • What made them special
  • How they influenced you
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I would like to talk about my mathematics teacher from secondary school, who had a profound impact on my approach to learning. Her name was Malika and she taught us for three years. What made her truly special was her ability to make complex topics feel simple and accessible — she had a talent for breaking down difficult concepts into clear, logical steps. She never made students feel embarrassed for asking questions, which created a very safe and supportive classroom environment. She also encouraged critical thinking by asking us not just to find the answer, but to explain our reasoning. Thanks to her influence, I developed a genuine love for problem-solving and academic study. I believe that great teachers don't just teach subjects — they inspire students to believe in their own potential.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How has education changed over the past few decades?
👁 Ko'rish
Education has transformed enormously. In the past, learning was almost entirely based on memorisation and textbooks. Today, critical thinking, creativity and digital skills are central to modern curricula. The rise of distance learning has also made education more accessible globally, breaking down geographical and financial barriers.
💡 Maslahat: Compare past and present clearly
2
Should education focus more on practical skills or academic knowledge?
👁 Ko'rish
I believe both are essential and they shouldn't be treated as opposites. Academic knowledge provides the theoretical foundation, while practical skills make that knowledge usable in real life. Vocational training is undervalued in many societies — we need more emphasis on applied skills alongside academic excellence.
💡 Maslahat: Balance your argument — avoid being one-sided
3
What role should governments play in education?
👁 Ko'rish
Governments have a fundamental responsibility to ensure compulsory education is free, high-quality and accessible to all, regardless of income or background. They should invest in teacher training, modern infrastructure, and inclusive education for students with disabilities. Additionally, providing scholarships for talented but disadvantaged students is crucial for social mobility.
💡 Maslahat: Use strong verbs: 'ensure', 'invest', 'provide', 'prioritise'
