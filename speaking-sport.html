🏃 Sport va Sog'liq

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
Do you do any sport or exercise regularly?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, I try to work out at least three times a week. I usually go jogging in the morning because it helps me maintain my stamina and keeps my mental health in good shape. I also do some basic stretching exercises at home.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'work out', 'keep fit', 'stay active' instead of just 'do sport'
2
What sport is most popular in your country?
👁 Ko'rish
Football is by far the most popular sport in Uzbekistan. People of all ages follow it passionately. However, boxing and wrestling are also widely practised, and Uzbek athletes have achieved great success in these sports at international competitions.
💡 Maslahat: Mention specific examples to sound more natural
3
Did you play any sports when you were a child?
👁 Ko'rish
When I was young, I was quite competitive and loved playing football with my friends after school. I also did some swimming classes, which helped me develop discipline and taught me the importance of endurance.
💡 Maslahat: Past tense — 'used to', 'would', 'when I was young'
4
Do you think it is important to be physically active?
👁 Ko'rish
Absolutely. Leading a sedentary lifestyle is one of the biggest health risks today. Regular physical activity not only improves your body but also boosts your mood and concentration. I believe a healthy lifestyle is essential for overall well-being.
💡 Maslahat: Give a strong opinion + reason + example
5
How do you feel after exercising?
👁 Ko'rish
After a good workout, I feel energised and much more focused. Even if I am tired from a long day, exercising gives me a natural energy boost. However, I always make sure to allow proper recovery time so I don't risk injury.
💡 Maslahat: Describe feelings and use cause-effect language
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a sport or physical activity you enjoy

  • What the sport/activity is
  • How often you do it
  • Who you do it with
  • Why you enjoy it
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I'd like to talk about jogging, which is a physical activity I genuinely enjoy. I go for a run every morning, usually for about 30 to 40 minutes, along the park near my home. I mostly jog alone, which gives me time to clear my mind and plan my day. What I enjoy most about it is the sense of freedom — there's no equipment needed and you can do it anywhere. Since I started jogging regularly, my stamina has improved significantly and my mental health has become much better. I feel more disciplined and energetic throughout the day. I would strongly recommend jogging to anyone looking for a simple yet highly effective form of exercise.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
Why do you think many people choose not to exercise despite knowing its benefits?
👁 Ko'rish
I think the main reason is the sedentary nature of modern life. Many people work long hours at desks and simply feel too exhausted to exercise afterwards. Additionally, the rise of social media and streaming services makes it easier to spend free time passively. Lack of motivation and immediate results also discourages beginners.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'I think/believe', 'the main reason is', 'additionally'
2
How has the attitude towards health and fitness changed in recent years?
👁 Ko'rish
In the past, health was mainly associated with the absence of illness. Today, however, people understand the importance of preventive health — maintaining fitness before problems arise. Social media has played a big role in promoting healthy lifestyles, though it has also created unrealistic body standards which can negatively affect mental health.
💡 Maslahat: Compare past and present — 'used to', 'nowadays', 'in contrast'
3
Should governments do more to encourage people to be physically active?
👁 Ko'rish
Definitely. Governments should invest in public sports facilities, promote physical activity in schools, and create awareness campaigns. Some countries have introduced incentives such as subsidised gym memberships or tax benefits for sports participation. This is not only beneficial for individuals but also reduces long-term healthcare costs for society.
💡 Maslahat: Give a balanced argument then state your view clearly
