📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
What is your favourite food and why?
👁 Ko'rish
My absolute favourite is plov — the traditional Uzbek rice dish. What makes it special is the combination of ingredients: fragrant rice cooked in lamb fat, tender meat, sweet carrots, and aromatic spices. The flavour is rich and deeply satisfying. Beyond the taste, plov carries cultural significance — it's a dish associated with celebration, community, and hospitality.
💡 Maslahat: Describe the food vividly — use sensory adjectives
2
Do you enjoy cooking? Why or why not?
👁 Ko'rish
I genuinely enjoy cooking, though I wouldn't call myself an expert. I find it a relaxing and creative activity after a long day. Following a recipe carefully and producing something nutritious and delicious is deeply satisfying. I particularly enjoy experimenting with different cuisines — recently I've been trying to cook some Asian dishes.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'I find it', 'I particularly enjoy', 'recently I've been'
3
Is the food in your country healthy?
👁 Ko'rish
Traditional Uzbek cuisine is quite nutritious — it's based on fresh ingredients, whole grains, and plenty of vegetables. However, modern eating habits are changing, with fast food and processed food becoming increasingly popular, particularly among young people. I think it's important to maintain the tradition of home-cooked meals and local produce.
💡 Maslahat: Distinguish traditional vs modern eating habits
4
Do you prefer eating at home or at a restaurant?
👁 Ko'rish
For everyday meals, I prefer eating at home — it's more nutritious, portions are controlled, and I know exactly what ingredients have been used. However, eating out is a wonderful social experience. Trying new cuisines at restaurants, particularly authentic street food, is one of my favourite ways to explore a new culture.
💡 Maslahat: Give different contexts for each preference
5
Have your eating habits changed in recent years?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, quite significantly. I've become much more conscious of eating a balanced diet and reducing my consumption of processed food. I now try to include more organic, locally sourced ingredients in my meals. I've also reduced my meat intake and explore more vegetarian options, primarily for health and environmental reasons.
💡 Maslahat: Show personal development: 'I've become', 'I now try', 'I've also'
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a meal or dish that is special to you

  • What the dish is
  • What ingredients it contains
  • When you usually eat it
  • Why it is special to you
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I would like to describe plov, which is the national dish of Uzbekistan and by far the most meaningful food in my life. Plov is made from rice, lamb, carrots, onions and a blend of spices, all cooked together in a large iron pot called a kazan. The flavour is extraordinarily rich — the rice absorbs all the aromas of the meat and spices during the long cooking process, creating something that is far greater than the sum of its ingredients. We usually eat plov at special occasions — weddings, celebrations, national holidays, and family gatherings. The preparation is itself a communal event: traditionally, the men of the family or community cook it together outdoors, which adds a social dimension to the experience. What makes plov truly special is that it represents Uzbek identity and hospitality. When you share plov with someone, it symbolises warmth, generosity and belonging. Every region of Uzbekistan has its own variation of the recipe, which reflects our country's rich culinary diversity. It is much more than food — it is a cultural experience.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How have eating habits changed in your country over the past few decades?
👁 Ko'rish
Eating habits in Uzbekistan have shifted considerably. Traditional home-cooked meals and local produce are being replaced by fast food, processed food, and international cuisines, particularly in urban areas. Busy lifestyles leave less time for cooking, and food delivery apps have made convenience the priority. While this offers variety, it has also raised concerns about nutrition and the loss of culinary traditions.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'has shifted', 'are being replaced' — present perfect and passive
2
Do you think governments should regulate what food companies can sell?
👁 Ko'rish
To a reasonable extent, yes. Governments have a responsibility to protect public health by setting standards for food safety and nutrition labelling. Restricting advertising of unhealthy fast food to children is particularly important. However, excessive regulation risks limiting consumer choice. The most effective approach combines clear information, incentives for healthier options, and education about balanced diets.
💡 Maslahat: Take a measured position — neither extreme
3
How serious is the problem of food waste globally?
👁 Ko'rish
Food waste is an enormous global problem. Approximately one third of all food produced is wasted, while millions of people face food insecurity. In wealthy countries, waste occurs largely at the consumer level — people buy too much and throw it away. Reducing food waste requires changes at every level: from smarter portion sizes in restaurants to better supply chain management and consumer education.
💡 Maslahat: Use statistics if possible, then offer solutions
