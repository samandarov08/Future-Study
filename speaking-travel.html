✈️ Sayohat

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
Do you enjoy travelling? Why or why not?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, I absolutely love travelling. There's something incredibly exciting about visiting new destinations and experiencing different cultures. Even if travel is sometimes tiring — especially the jet lag after long flights — the memories and experiences you gain are completely worth it.
💡 Maslahat: Express enthusiasm + acknowledge a downside to sound balanced
2
Where have you travelled to, and which place did you like most?
👁 Ko'rish
I have visited several cities within Uzbekistan, including Samarkand and Bukhara, which are truly breathtaking for their historical landmarks and scenic beauty. If I had to choose, Samarkand was my favourite — the Registan is one of the most stunning architectural sites I have ever seen.
💡 Maslahat: Be specific — name real places and describe them
3
Do you prefer travelling alone or with others?
👁 Ko'rish
Both have their advantages. Travelling alone offers complete freedom to follow your own itinerary and pace. Backpacking solo also builds confidence and independence. However, sharing experiences with friends or family creates lasting memories that are simply more enjoyable.
💡 Maslahat: Use 'both have advantages', 'however', 'on the other hand'
4
What is the most important thing to prepare before a trip?
👁 Ko'rish
Research is key. Before any trip, I carefully plan my accommodation, check visa requirements, and research the local culture to avoid culture shock. I also prepare a rough itinerary — not too strict, but enough to ensure I don't miss the most important landmarks.
💡 Maslahat: List 2-3 preparations with brief reasons
5
Would you rather visit natural places or cities?
👁 Ko'rish
I tend to prefer cities because of the rich history, architecture, and variety of experiences they offer. However, I also appreciate off the beaten path natural destinations — remote, scenic places away from crowds have a special kind of beauty that cities simply can't replicate.
💡 Maslahat: Use contrasting language to show a nuanced view
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a memorable journey or trip you have taken

  • Where you went
  • Who you went with
  • What you did there
  • Why it was memorable
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I'd like to describe a trip I took to Samarkand with my family last summer, which was truly one of the most memorable experiences of my life. We spent three days exploring the city's extraordinary historical sites. The highlight was visiting the Registan, which is a magnificent square surrounded by three stunning madrasas dating back to the 15th century. The sheer scale and beauty of the architecture left me speechless. We also tried the incredible local cuisine — plov, samsa and shashlik — which was unlike anything I had tasted before. What made the trip particularly special was the time spent together as a family, away from the routine of daily life. I came home with wonderful souvenirs and a much deeper appreciation for Uzbekistan's rich cultural heritage. I would highly recommend Samarkand to anyone who appreciates history and architecture.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How has international tourism changed in recent years?
👁 Ko'rish
Tourism has changed dramatically. Low-cost airlines and online booking platforms have made travel more accessible than ever before. However, this has led to over-tourism in popular destinations, damaging local environments and communities. The pandemic also forced the industry to rethink sustainability and hygiene standards.
💡 Maslahat: Mention both opportunities and challenges
2
Do you think tourism is beneficial or harmful to local communities?
👁 Ko'rish
Tourism can be enormously beneficial — it creates jobs, boosts local economies, and helps preserve cultural heritage. However, it can also be harmful if poorly managed. Mass tourism can drive up living costs for locals, damage historical sites, and create tension between visitors and residents. Sustainable, responsible tourism is the ideal balance.
💡 Maslahat: Give both sides then suggest a solution
3
Will people travel more or less in the future?
👁 Ko'rish
I believe people will continue to travel, but the nature of travel will change. Environmental concerns may encourage shorter, more meaningful trips rather than frequent long-haul flights. Virtual reality tourism may become an alternative for some. However, the fundamental human desire to experience new places and cultures will never disappear.
💡 Maslahat: Make predictions: 'I believe', 'it is likely that', 'may', 'will'
