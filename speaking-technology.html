💻 Texnologiya

IELTS Speaking · Part 1, 2, 3 · Namuna javoblar bilan

📝 Part 1 — Shaxsiy savollar

O'zingiz haqingizda gapirib bering

1
How often do you use technology in your daily life?
👁 Ko'rish
I use technology constantly throughout the day. My devices — smartphone, laptop and earphones — are essential for studying, communicating and entertainment. I rely heavily on digital tools for learning, from watching educational videos to using apps for language practice. It's hard to imagine a day without them.
💡 Maslahat: Use frequency adverbs: constantly, frequently, regularly, occasionally
2
What is your favourite piece of technology and why?
👁 Ko'rish
My favourite piece of technology is definitely my smartphone. It combines so many functions in one small device — communication, navigation, education and entertainment. The connectivity it provides means I'm always in touch with friends and family, regardless of distance.
💡 Maslahat: Give a reason + example for every point
3
Do you think people spend too much time on their phones?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, I think screen time has become a serious issue. Many people, especially younger generations, show signs of addiction — constantly checking notifications even during conversations or meals. It's important to set boundaries and take regular breaks from screens for better mental health.
💡 Maslahat: Express an opinion then support it with evidence
4
Have you ever had a problem with technology?
👁 Ko'rish
Yes, actually quite recently. My laptop crashed during an important assignment and I lost several hours of work. It made me realise the importance of regular backups and cybersecurity. Since then I've been much more careful about saving my files and updating my software.
💡 Maslahat: Use past narrative: 'it made me realise', 'since then I've been'
5
Is technology used effectively in education in your country?
👁 Ko'rish
It's improving, but there's still a long way to go. During the pandemic, online education became necessary, which accelerated the adoption of digital tools. However, access to technology is still unequal — students in rural areas often lack reliable internet connectivity and modern devices.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge both positives and challenges
🎤 Part 2 — Uzoq monolog

1-2 daqiqa gapiring

Describe a piece of technology you find very useful

  • What it is
  • How you use it
  • How often you use it
  • Why it is important to you
1
Namuna javobni ko'ring
👁 Ko'rish
I'd like to describe my laptop, which is undoubtedly the most useful piece of technology in my life. I use it every single day for studying, doing research, and working on projects. It allows me to access educational platforms, write assignments and communicate with teachers and classmates. What makes it particularly valuable is the way it combines so many tools in one — I can use spreadsheets, design programmes and video conferencing software all in one place. Since acquiring it, my productivity has improved dramatically. Before, I had to rely on my phone for everything, which was quite inefficient. I think digital tools like laptops are essential in today's world, especially for students who need to manage large amounts of information and meet tight deadlines.
💬 Part 3 — Muhokama

Chuqurroq fikrlash va baholash

1
How has technology changed the way people communicate?
👁 Ko'rish
Technology has transformed communication beyond recognition. In the past, people relied on letters and landlines. Today, social media, messaging apps and video calls allow instant communication across the globe. However, some argue this has made communication more superficial — people interact digitally but feel more isolated in real life.
💡 Maslahat: Use contrast language: 'in the past... today, however'
2
Do you think artificial intelligence will replace human jobs?
👁 Ko'rish
Artificial intelligence and automation will undoubtedly replace many routine jobs, especially in manufacturing and data processing. However, I believe creativity, emotional intelligence and complex problem-solving will remain uniquely human. The challenge for society is to prepare workers for this shift through education and retraining programmes.
💡 Maslahat: Acknowledge both sides then give your conclusion
3
What are the main risks associated with increasing use of technology?
👁 Ko'rish
The most significant risks include privacy concerns — companies collect vast amounts of personal data through algorithms. There is also the risk of cybersecurity breaches, misinformation spread through social media, and growing addiction to digital devices among young people. Governments and tech companies must work together to address these challenges.
💡 Maslahat: List 2-3 risks with brief explanations
